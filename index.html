Passage 1: The Lighthouse Keeper's Dog

by M. J. Carroll
1Old Remy climbed the lighthouse stairEach night before the stars appeared,And Biscuit climbed right with him there,A dog both brave and dearly feared.
5"Now Biscuit, stay," old Remy said,Each time he lit the great lamp's flame."The light must shine or ships may dreadThe rocks, and nothing stays the same."
9But Biscuit never stayed below.He pressed his nose against the glassAnd watched the steady golden glowSwing out across the waves like brass.
13One winter night the lamp went dark —Old Remy slipped and hurt his knee.A ship came sailing toward the markOf hidden rocks beneath the sea.
17Then Biscuit barked — a long, loud soundThat rang across the water's face.The sailors heard, and turning round,They steered the ship to safer space.
21Old Remy heard it from the floorAnd dragged himself up, step by step.He relit the lamp once more,While Biscuit's golden head was kept
25Upon his master's lap that night,As if to say, "I kept the light."
"The Lighthouse Keeper's Dog" by M. J. Carroll. Original work.
