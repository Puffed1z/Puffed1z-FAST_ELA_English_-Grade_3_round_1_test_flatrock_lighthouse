Passage 2: The Keeper of Flat Rock Cove

by M. J. Carroll
devoted: having very strong love or loyalty toward someone or something
1Every morning before school, eleven-year-old Dara walked the beach at Flat Rock Cove to pick up trash. She called it her "shift," as if she were a real worker clocking in.
2"Why do you bother?" her friend Sasha asked one day, watching Dara pull a plastic bag from a tide pool. "You can't clean the whole ocean."
3"I'm not trying to clean the ocean," Dara said. "I'm trying to clean this beach." She held up the bag and dropped it into her bucket. "One bag at a time."
4Sasha rolled her eyes, but she picked up an old bottle cap and dropped it in the bucket too.
5That spring a loggerhead sea turtle came ashore at Flat Rock Cove — a rare sight. Because the beach was clean, the turtle found a clear patch of sand and nested there. Wildlife officers came and roped off the area.
6"It's because of you," one officer told Dara. "Sea turtles don't nest on polluted beaches. You made this possible."
7Dara stood at the edge of the roped-off area and looked at the smooth mound of sand where the turtle had laid her eggs. She thought of every bag and bottle cap she had ever picked up, every early morning, every gray drizzly day.
8Sasha appeared beside her. "Okay," Sasha said quietly. "I get it now."
9The next morning, Sasha showed up at the beach before Dara did, bucket in hand, already working her way down the shoreline.
10Dara was devoted to the cove — and now, it seemed, the cove had given something back.
